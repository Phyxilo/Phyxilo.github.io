‌PUBLICATIONS

[2023]

Proceedings of Science - NA65(DsTau): Study of Tau Neutrino Production in p-A Interactions This Proceedings of Science publication, presented at the European Physical Society Conference on High Energy Physics in Hamburg, Germany, highlights the DsTau experiment at CERN-SPS. The study focuses on measuring the differential cross-section of Ds production and its decay to tau leptons in proton-nucleus interactions, utilizing nuclear emulsion detectors for high-resolution detection of decay topologies. The publication covered the physics potential of the experiment, analysis results from the pilot run, and future plans.

[2023]

Development of proton beam irradiation system for the NA65/DsTau experiment This article details the development of the target mover, a new proton irradiation system for the NA65/DsTau experiment at CERN. The target mover ensures uniform proton distribution across the detector surface, achieving a density of 1.01 × 105 cm-2 with a fluctuation of just 1.9 ± 0.3%. Target mover provides optimal conditions for the reconstruction of interactions, enhancing the precision of the experiment.

[2024]

Study of Proton-Nucleus Interactions in the DsTau/NA65 Experiment at the CERN-SPS This publication presents the primary proton interactions at 400 GeV in the CERN-SPS facility. Using a new reconstruction method, the study achieved high precision in selecting primary interactions, and the findings, including the testing of KNO-G scaling, provide valuable data for validating Monte Carlo event generators and advancing our understanding of primary proton interactions.

[2024]

Proceedings of Science - NA65(DsTau): Study of tau neutrino production in p-A interactions This Proceedings of Science publication, presented at the 31st International Workshop on Deep Inelastic Scattering in Grenoble, France, outlines the findings from the DsTau (NA65) experiment at CERN. The research focuses on measuring the differential cross-section of Ds production and decay branching ratios in proton-nucleus interactions, using the nuclear emulsion technique for precise vertex reconstruction. The study's results, including the measurement of proton interaction length and the testing of KNO-G scaling, provide crucial data for validating Monte Carlo event generators.
